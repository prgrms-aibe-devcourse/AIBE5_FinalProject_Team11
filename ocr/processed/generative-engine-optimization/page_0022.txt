6.3 The Power of Citing and Being Cited
Trust is transitive in the eyes of an AI.
Cite Authoritative Sources: When you make a claim, link out to
the original source —a university study, a government report, a re-
spected industry publication. This shows that you engage with the
expert community and ground your work in facts. Build High-Qual-
ity Backlinks: The core of traditional SEO is still vital. Earning links
from other authoritative websites is the ultimate vote of confidence.
It tells generative engines that other experts trust your content.
Focus on quality, not quantity. One link from a top-tier industry blog
is worth more than 100 links from low-quality directories.
6.4 Case Study - www.kunalguha.com
I practice what I preach. I've structured my own website, www.kun-
alguha.com, as a small case study in building an E-E-A-T profile for
an individual expert.
Homepage: It clearly states who I am ("13-Year Digital Marketing
Veteran") and what I do. It establishes my expertise from the first
second.
About Page: This serves as my primary Person schema entity page.
It details my 13+ years of experience, mentions specific career mile-
stones, and establishes my philosophy.
Blog/Insights: The content I publish is designed to be a "Source of
Truth," incorporating data, unique insights from my career, and ac-
tionable advice—just like this book. Every post has a clear author bio.
External Footprint: If you search my name, you'll find my LinkedIn
profile, articles I've written for other publications, and mentions
from industry events. This creates a cohesive web of authority that
search engines can easily piece together.
