Employing Fact-First Sentence Structures
One highly effective technique involves adopting Fact-
First sentence structures. This means beginning sen-
tences with the core assertion or data point, followed
by supporting details. For example, instead of writing,
"Many factors contribute to climate change, with car-
bon emissions being the primary cause," rephrase it as,
"The primary cause of climate change is carbon emis-
sions."
This direct approach reduces cognitive load for the
LLM, making it easier to extract and attribute specific
pieces of information. Consider how an LLM processes
text; it prioritizes declarative statements as potential
answers. By front-loading your facts, you essentially
prepackage the citation-worthy segments of your con-
tent.
