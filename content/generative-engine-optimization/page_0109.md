Adapting to Claude's Long-Form Preference
Claude's architecture excels at processing and synthe-
sizing information from longer, more detailed docu-
ments. Data shows Claude citations favor sources with
500-800 word deepdives, suggesting that comprehen-
sive articles are more likely to be selected as authori-
tative references. Avoid overly concise or fragmented
content when targeting Claude's retrieval.
Developing content with a clear narrative flow and
logical progression of ideas benefits Claude's ability to
extract context. Use strong topic sentences and well-
defined paragraphs to segment information, making
the entire document more digestible for the generative
model. This structured approach helps Claude under-
stand the full scope of your argument.
When crafting content for Claude, focus on provid-
ing thorough explanations and supporting evidence
within the document itself. Unlike Gemini, which
might pull from disparate Google sources, Claude often
seeks to find complete answers within a single, exten-
sive resource. This emphasizes the importance of creat-
ing canonical content that stands alone.
